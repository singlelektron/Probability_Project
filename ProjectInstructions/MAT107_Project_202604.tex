\section{Problem 1 -- Drawing balls with replacement}
 
	You shall consider the following experiment. In an urn are $m$ white balls and $n$ black balls  -- each group will get their values for $m$ and $n$ in a separate email. A ball is drawn randomly, its colour is noted and then the ball is placed back in the urn. The draw is repeated, the colour of the ball is noted and the ball is placed back, and so on. In total 20 balls are drawn.
 
	Calculate the probability that a white ball is drawn. Based on this probability, simulate the experiment 500 times using excel -- use the provided excel sheet. 
 
	Your tasks are as follows.
	\begin{enumerate}
 		\item Find the percentage of white balls in all your 10000 draws. Compare it with the expected values. Give a short explanation of the theoretical background.
 		\item Let $X$ be the number of white balls drawn. Find the probability mass function for this random variable. Then, find the relative frequency of the number of white balls drawn in your 500 experiments.
 		Compare them with the calculated probabilities. Present the results in a table (you can break the table into two parts side by side). Give a short explanation of the theoretical background.

 			\hline 
 			1 & & \\
 			\hline 
 			2 & & \\
 			\hline 
 			3 & & \\
 			\hline 
 		\end{tabular} \begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|}
 		\hline 
 		$x$ & $p(x)$ & rel. freq. \\ 
 		\hline 
 		11 & & \\
 		\hline 
 		12 & & \\
 		\hline 
 		13 & & \\
 		\hline 
 		\end{tabular} 
 		
 		
 		
 		\item Find the expected value of $X$, and find the average of the number of white balls drawn in your 500 experiments. Compare the two values and discuss them. 
 		\item Find the probability that there is no white ball among the first 3 balls drawn. Then, find the relative frequency of your experiments that do not have a white ball among the first three balls drawn. Compare the values and discuss them.  Numerical values shall have a reasonable number of digits. For the normal distribution, use excel to calculate the exact value. Then, solve the problem using the table for the standard normal distribution and discuss the relative error with the exact value for the normal distribution.
 
 
 
 \section{Problem 2 -- Drawing balls without replacement}
 
 You shall consider a problem similar to the previous one, but now you shall draw balls without replacement. Again, the urn contains $m$ white balls and $n$ black balls  -- use the same values you have got for Problem~1. Here, a ball is drawn randomly, but now it is not replaced. Then a second ball is drawn (but not replaced) and so on. In total 20 balls are drawn.
 
 Calculate the probability that a white ball is drawn, given that already $m_1$ white balls and $n_1$ black balls have been drawn. Based on this probability, simulate the experiment 500 times using excel -- use the provided excel sheet. 
 
 Here, you have to inform yourself about the hypergeometric distribution - consult the textbook.
 
 Your tasks are as follows.
 \begin{enumerate}
 	\item Let $X$ be the number of white balls drawn. Find the probability mass function for this random variable. Then, find the relative frequency of the number of white balls drawn in your 500 experiments.
 	Compare them with the calculated probabilities. Present the results in a table. Give a short explanation of the theoretical background.
 	\item Find the expected value of $X$, and find the average of the number of white balls drawn in your 500 experiments. Compare the two values and discuss them. 
 	\item Find the probability that there is no white ball among the first 3 balls drawn. Then, find the relative frequency of your experiments that do not have a white ball among the first three balls drawn. Compare the values and discuss them. Give a short explanation of the theoretical background.
 \end{enumerate}
 
 For each of the three tasks, compare your results with the corresponding results of Problem~1 and discuss them.
